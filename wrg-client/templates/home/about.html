About

WorkReadyGrad elevates the dialogue between the supply and demand side of our economy (academia and industry), connecting students directly with employers to learn the real-world talent sought, enabling them to proactively acquire the skills and experiences necessary to become a good fit job candidate by the time they graduate from college and technical schools.

This tool was initially built in 2012 by Georgia Tech students in their CS Capstone Design Class with Dr. Bob Waters and led by Tech alumnus, Brian Srikanchna (BME '08).

Our first pilot was in the Biomedical Engineering Department of Georgia Tech with over 150+ students and 30+ employers participating, including Home Depot, Coca-Cola, GE Healthcare, P&G, CardioMEMS, Citrix, and Edwards Life Sciences.Presently we're building a high school version of WorkReadyGrad to prepare students for admission into their college or technical school of choice.

This tool directly address the Skills Gap and Student Graduation Rates.
